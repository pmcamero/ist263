Why I am Qualified to talk about Linguistics

At Syracuse University, I am an International Relations and Linguistics Studies double major with a minor in Information Management and Technology. I have spent a good portion of my undergraduate career studying linguistics and taking linguistics courses. As a junior at Syracuse, I have almost finished my linguistics major and I have written a research paper for essentially every linguistics course that I have taken.

Some courses at Syracuse that I have taken:
  • LIN 201: Nature & Study of Language
  • LIN 371: Bilingualism and Multiculturalism
  • LIN 373: Language Variation and Change
  • LIN 301: Introduction to Linguistic Analysis
  • LIN 305: Linguistic Structure of English

Linguistics courses that I am currently enrolled in:
  • LIN 375: Forensic Linguistics
  • LIN 431: Phonological Analysis
  • LIN 441: Syntactic Analysis

For every class, but LIN 201, LIN 301, and LIN 375, to finish the course there was a final paper that I had to write. For each paper, I wrote either a library-based research paper or a research proposal.

The list of topics that I wrote are:
  • Research Proposal for LIN 371 on Bilingual Education and the Effect on Social Interaction in Young Children
  • Research Proposal for LIN 373 on The Effect of Social Media on the Variation of English
  • Library Based Research Paper for LIN 305 on How Emoji Use is Affected by and changes with age
