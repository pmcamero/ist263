Why I am Qualified to talk about Linguistics

A bit about me!

At Syracuse University, I am an International Relations and Linguistics Studies double major with a minor in Information Management and Technology.

I have spent a good portion of my undergraduate career studying linguistics and taking linguistics courses. As a junior at Syracuse, I have almost finished my linguistics major and I have written a research paper for essentially every linguistics course that I have taken.

Additionally, summer of 2021 I will be researching on Jarawan languages, which originate in Nigeria, via recordings of native speakers who now live in Syracuse. Throughout this summer I will be collecting a lexicon and comparing Jarawan to other languages in the surrounding area of Nigeria.

Some courses at Syracuse that I have taken:

  • LIN 201: Nature & Study of Language
  • LIN 371: Bilingualism and Multiculturalism
  • LIN 373: Language Variation and Change
  • LIN 301: Introduction to Linguistic Analysis
  • LIN 305: Linguistic Structure of English

Linguistics courses that I am currently enrolled in:

  • LIN 375: Forensic Linguistics
  • LIN 431: Phonological Analysis
  • LIN 441: Syntactic Analysis

For every class, but LIN 201, LIN 301, and LIN 375, to finish the course there was a final paper that I had to write. For each paper, I wrote either a library-based research paper or a research proposal.
